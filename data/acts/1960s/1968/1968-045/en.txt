£, D—O. 12/67.

‘AN ACT TO ENABLE EFFECT TO BE GIVEN TO CERTAIN
PROVISIONS OF THE CHARTER OF THE UNITED NaTIONs.
[Date of Assent: December 14, 1968]

BE it enacted by the Queen’s Most Excellent Majesty,
by and with the advice and consent of the Senate and
the House of Representatives of Ceylon in this present
Parliament assembled, and by the authority of the

‘same, as follows: —

4. This Act may be cited as the United Nations short tite.
Act, No. 45 of 1968.

2. (1) If, under Article forty-one of the Charter of Measures
the United Nations signed at San Francisco on the ji)" *""
twenty-sixth day of June, Nineteen hundred and
Forty-five, (being the Article which relates to measures
not involving the use of armed force) the Security
Council of the United Nations call upon the Govern-
ment of Ceylon to apply any measures to give effect to
any decision of that Council, the Minister of Defence
and External Affairs may by regulations make such
provision as appears to him necessary or expedient for
enabling those measures to be eflectively applied,
including (without prejudice to the generality of the
preceding provisions of this sub-section) provision for
the apprehension, trial and punishment of persons
offending against the regulations.

(2) Every regulation made under the preceding
provisions of this section shall, forthwith after it is
made, be tabled in the Senate and the House of
Representatives.

(3) Any expenses incurred by the Government of
Ceylon in applying any such measures as are referred
to in the preceding provisions of this section shall be
defrayed out of moneys provided by Parliament for
the purpose.
